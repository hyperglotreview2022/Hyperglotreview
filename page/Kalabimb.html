Kalabimb (Visual Art Gallery)

Kalabimb-24 is an exhibition of the finest works from 24 well established artists from all over India, showcasing the diverse and dynamic expressions of contemporary art. This year's collection bringstogether a vibrant array of artists, each pushing the boundaries of creativity through innovative techniques and thought-provoking themes.
Finetouch Art Motivators & Educators (FAME) conceiving with a view to encourage a rejuvenation of spirit within the visual art community. Art is an expression of emotion and the feeling deep within one's heart. We thought that all art enthusiasts may enjoy and at the same time provide an added avenue for the artists to show their works in their visual languages as Paintings, Digital, Photographs and mix media.
Indian art continually stirred by a long history of engagement with the practice of making art and of facing complex relationships with forces from within and beyond the country. Intense migration, exceptional mixture, precocious mastery, devotion to image and commitment to its critique, and a sentimental affection for the folk and the curiosity to mediate the western and modern - these are only some of the contexts of the art of the current time and these likewise implicate the currency of recognition as Indian art circulates quite actively in the region and elsewhere.
The diversity of Indian art became more robust by permeated artistic practice and cut across the realms of the personal and the political. The state of the earth, the position of women in society, the changing landscape of cities, the vexing spectre of identity were some of the most cogent themes and concerns informing the work of artists.
"FAME" curate group exhibitions in which artists revolves around the theme of "Interconnected Realities." In an era where the digital and physical realms increasingly intersect, this exhibition explores how artists navigate and merge these spaces. The artworks featured interrogate the complexities of identity, technology, and environment, reflecting the multifaceted experiences of our contemporary world.

Anita Goyal

Technically trained in fine arts, creatively inclined, dabbling in everything from flower arrangement to painting home furnishings commercially. Her niche is in abstract expressionism, inspired by post war modernism, and how the field borrows from abstract art floating between cubism, impressionism and expressionism. This collection is inspired by post war impressionists and artists of that era. Following the example of J.M.W turner and Mark Rothko who constructed their pictures from freely brushed colours, that took precedence over lines and contours. It is this movement that inspired the artist Anita to adopt the style and blend it with her abstract art.

Anjali Gupta

Anjali Gupta is an accomplished artist basically painting abstract expressionism form. She plays with colours contemplating on thoughts and feelings and giving a meaning to the abstract art. With a stint at Triveni Kala Sangam, Delhi, she added more depth and gestures with imagination and bold strokes. She tries to send a message to society through her art. She has had many exhibitions in leading galleries of Delhi NCR like Visual Art Gallery Habitat Centre, AIFACS, Artizen Gallery, RKG, Gandhi Art Gallery etc. She has worked in Advertising, Marketing and Education industry and has about 19 years of experience. With a Masters and honorary PhD degree in Mass Communication, she teaches in colleges of Delhi NCR.

Dr. Suryasnata Mohanty

Suryasnata Mohanty is an accomplished contemporary artist, whose works have been shown in the India Art Architecture & Design Biennale 2023 at Red Fort and in prestigious group shows at NGMA, Lalit Kala Akademi, Visual Arts Gallery, The Lalit, Salarjung Museum Hyderabad, Pracheen Kala Kendra Chandigarh, Tagore National Exhibition Bhopal etc, besides in solo shows at India Habitat Centre and DLF Promenade, New Delhi. She featured in the reputed art journal 'The Indian Creative Minds' September 2022 issue. Famously, the 'Kalash' (clay pot) painted by Suryasnata in a Lalit Kala Akademi camp at Garhi for 'Amrit Kalash Mahotsav' got selected and was held by Hon'ble PM of India to culminate the nationwide 'Meri Maati Mera Desh' campaign at India Gate in New Delhi in October 2023. Subsequently she became the first visual artist to receive the "Delhi Star Award 2023" in 'The Innovator of the Year' category. She is also a recipient of "Rashtriya Rajat Samman" of Kalavart Nyas, Ujjain, MP, during its silver jubilee international art camp in Dec 2023.

Fulla Chandra Mistry

Despite his unassuming appearance, he possesses a prodigious talent for innovation, brimming with a supernatural ability to transmute imagination into tangible reality through his artistry. His canvases breathe with the essence of his own ideology, a harmonious blend of traditional finesse and contemporary vision. Each stroke of his brush is imbued with the whispers of rural landscapes and the pulse of urban life, creating a tapestry that resonates with viewers on a profound level.

Himani Pasricha

Himani Pasricha, a visionary artist, masterfully intertwines abstract painting with the profound themes of life and chakras. Through her captivating use of color theory, she navigates the intricate land- scapes of human emotions. Her canvases are alive with ships and boats, symbolic vessels that sail through the tumultuous seas of our emotional experiences. Each brushstroke and hue is meticulously chosen to reflect the journey of overcoming worries and achieving emotional harmony. Himani's work is not just a visual feast but a therapeutic voyage, inviting viewers to explore their inner selves and find solace. Her art resonates deeply, offering a unique blend of aesthetic beauty and spiritual insight, making her a distinguished figure in the contemporary art scene.

Krishna Karmeshvar

Krishna's "Ananda" inspires my art, seen in the delicate dance of flowers. His boundless love transcends all, embracing life's essence. Each petal, though seemingly ephemeral, embodies the essence of Krishna himself. They sway in a drunken ecstasy, mirroring the joyous abandon of the divine. Every blossom, with its unique beauty, whispers of Krishna's presence, inviting the beholder to join the eternal quest for enlightenment. As the gopis were bewitched by Krishna's charm, so too are our eyes ensnared by the enchanting allure of nature's splendor. In the intricate tapestry of petals and stems, we glimpse the eternal dance of creation, where war and peace converge in a symphony of harmonious contradiction.

Manomoy Das

TManomoy's artworks are a mesmerizing fusion of Cubism and monochrome, each piece a testament to his distinctive style. With a keen eye for specific themes, his compositions are imbued with an underlying Indian Folk perspective, adding depth and resonance to his creations. Predominantly utilizing oil on canvas as his medium of choice, Manomoy expertly manipulates color, form, and texture to evoke a sense of intrigue and introspection in the viewer. His paintings transcend mere aesthetic appeal, offering a glimpse into the rich tapestry of cultural heritage while simultaneously exploring the complexities of the human experience. Through his unique blend of artistic influences, Manomoy invites us to reconsider familiar narratives and discover new depths of meaning within his captivating works of art.

Mannish Rao

Mannish Rao Jwade's suite of meticulous paintings promises its viewers a phantasmagorical journey through imaginary lands populated by strange architectural forms, metallic beings and dreamlike situations. The artist here is a raconteur, spinning fables that reach into his own subconscious to createa palimpsest effect. Rao cunningly hides objects within objects and tales within tales in his colorful works. His stories divined from diverse sources such as child- hood narrations of kingly valour to contemporary social crisis clamor for attention from within the claustrophobic space of the drawings.

Mohd. Danish

As an artist, Danish is fascinated by the complex workings of the human mind, and how memories and experiences shape our perceptions of the world around us. Through his work, He seek to capture the ephemeral nature of memory and the powerful emotions that nostalgia can evoke. Using digital tools and techniques, Mohd Danish has created a series of images titled "Jaun Ke Baad". These are the powerful and emotionally resonant works of art that invites contemplation and introspection. The artist has succeeded in translating the poetic verse of Jaun Elia into a visual narrative that speaks to the universal human condition, reminding us of the timeless dance between desire and mortality, love and loss.

Paramananda Choudhury

Nature is his passion and inspiration. The colours, tones and textures are derived from the perception of senses. Greatest of the artists have owed their inspiration to the beauty of the inimitable creations and manifestations of nature. The illumination, the revelations (the more stated, the lesser) that nature offers, have been the life force of life itself. The surreal landscapes imbued with a spirit of the mighty relics add a force to his work. Artists romance with the relics of monuments and their mammoth impact on the human eye brought his frenzy to be one with nature to its culmination. In the present series he brings out this divine impact of relics and nature on mankind. Nature has touched the lives of people with its power and force, it enfuses the same power in humans in adversetimes and once again energises them.

Priyanka Bardhan

Priyanka Bardhan, currently based out of New Delhi, India, is a professional Artist with the belief that art is the connection to humanity and a way to channelize the divinity within. She had an inclination for creativity right from her childhood, most of which was spent in Kolkata West Bengal, India. She did political science honors graduate from Calcutta University and had been associated with Birla Academy of Art and Culture Kolkata. Priyanka predominantly use acrylic on canvas, pencil sketch and mixed media for her artistic expressions. Her art works are based on Indian contemporary painting styles and themes since these are not just a reflection of the indigenous lifestyle but a perfect example of artistic expression through simple yet distinct compositions, she also try to experiment by blending and fusing the indian traditional style and themes with abstract art and cubism styles to give a unique impression of my own. She have been participating in various online and offline exhibitions off-late, in order to showcase my experiments and learn from the contemporary styles being practiced.

Ravi Kant Jha

Ravi's artistic journey is a delicate fusion of ancient myth and contemporary expression. I intertwine Indian mythological tales with modern contexts, guiding viewers through semi- abstract narratives that unfold elegantly. Before I paint, I meticulously lay down foundations, nurturing the creation process. Lines are central to my expression, carrying their own fluid language, leading viewers through intricate yet familiar forms. Color evokes emotion and mood, layered to create harmonious symphonies that captivate. Texture adds depth, inviting tactile engagement, from delicate washes to bold impasto strokes. Transparency layers create luminosity, inviting exploration of the artwork's many meanings. Each piece aims to elevate the visual experience, blending myth and modernity seamlessly. My art reflects a dialogue between past and present, celebrating Indian culture and mythology through the lens of my imagination.

Ravindra Kr. Tanwar

He's a creative soul with a lens as his paintbrush, capturing the essence of life in the seemingly mundane. His journey as a photographer is a fusion of his trained eye as a graphic designer and his passion for motivating others through art. For him, photography isn't just about snapping pictures; it's about unleashing his intense creative energy and sharing the stories he sees through his lens. With a keen eye for beauty in the ordinary, he transforms everyday scenes into captivating visual narratives. Through the interplay of light, texture, and color, he crafts images that invite viewers into a world of subtle complexity and unexpected allure. His photographs aren't just about places; they're about the people who inhabit them. For him, photography is more than just a hobby or a profession; it's a way to connect with people and cultures, to explore the depths of human experience, and to create lasting memories of shared moments. Through his lens, he seeks not only to capture images but also to evoke emotions and spark curiosity about the world around us.

Ritesh Bagai

Ritesh Bagai, commonly known as 'An Indian Miniaturist', hails from Delhi, India. Art has been his lifelong passion, inherited from his mother. With a master's degree in Graphic Design from Coventry University, UK, he currently serves as an art director at Mullen Lowe Lintas. His artistic niche lies in miniature art. Ritesh captures minute details, crafting intricate pieces that demand meticulous attention. Painting these tiny artworks serves as a meditative retreat for him. Utilizing water colors and the tiniest of brushes, he immerse himself in the world of intricate creations. Recently, he has been exploring conceptual themes in his work, adding depth to his artistic repertoire.

Undoubtedly, environment is constantly changing and it demands attention else this Man-made Environment will swallow up the Geographical Environment. Hence, the idea behind of the art is to make aware the folks about the necessity of a well- balanced atmosphere and nurturing it as much as 'WE CAN'. His main components in casting of Sculpture Art are purely 95% based on metals wire which intensively bend and mould by bare hands only. Sunil Jaiswal

Sunil Jaiswal, was born in 1969 and brought up in the city of Delhi, known as the 'city of lovers'. His childhood was a fun-filled one with lots of colors. Ever since he was a young boy, Sunil was always interested in painting. He would participate in competitions and win them. One worth mentioning is the 'On the Spot Competition on Mother and Child' organized by the Anuvart Bhavan. He won first prize and the trophy was presented to Sunil by the former president of India. Besides art, he was also very much interested in photography. Sunil was so fascinated by the beauty of art that he couldn't deny the fact that he was in love with it. As he learned every stroke, each line, he was more and more attracted to the world of art. In 1993, after his four-year painting course, he continued this journey and received a BA degree in fine arts. Here he came in contact with a variety of art media and enjoyed every bit of it. Sunil don't want to be bound by any particular theme or style and have tried his hand at almost everything connected with art, like wood sculpting, clay molding, metal, fiberglass, wrought iron, ceramics, etc. he did quite a bit of work in wrought iron and metal sculpting and got a good response. He love working on Ganesha themes and have done quite a lot of pieces on this deity. Sunil's theme depends on his mood and keeps changing with time. Right now he is working on music meditation. Here he explore human involvement with music in any form. he want to portray the joy people derive from meditation.
