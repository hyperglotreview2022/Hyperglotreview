Interfaces

Image Credit - Hyperglot Review

INTERFACES is HOLZIndia's first exhibition in the country. HOLZIndia has been created through a collaboration between two groundbreaking art projects: HOLZ and INFUSED STORY . The event will be held at Gallerie Romain Rolland , Alliance Française de Delhi , Lodhi Road, New Delhi in association with Alliance Française de Delhi , the Embassy of France , the Embassy of Belgium , and Lalit Kala Akademi , New Delhi . During the exhibition's inauguration, Stephane Alamir , Director Alliance Française de Delhi will greet our esteemed guests from the Embassy of Belgium who will be represented at the show, along with the representatives of our partners and sponsors.

Ananda Moy Banerji

Ananda after completing his bachelor of Fine Arts in Painting from college of art Delhi 1975-1980, and then master in Fine Arts in Printmaking from Kala Bhavan - Santiniketan 1983-1985, went to Camberwell college of Arts and Crafts-London for research associate ship in Printmaking under the Charles Wallace India trust award. He has exhibited in numerous shows in India and abroad including number of Solo shows in - Gallery Espace, The British Council and Lalit Kala Akademi , India Habitat Centre,New Delhi, and many other galleries in various cities.

Anita Das Chakraborty

Anita Das Chakraborty was a talented artist known for her work in printmaking. She completed her Master of Fine Arts (MFA) in printmaking and dedicated herself to this art form throughout her career. Her prints were showcased in many national and international exhibitions, including the XIII Biennale internationale and the 1st Print Biennale organized by the Lalit Kala Academy in 2018.

Dattatraya Apte

Dattatraya Apte is an accomplished artist known for his profound exploration of surfaces and abstract impressions. He began his artistic journey by obtaining a Diploma in Drawing and Painting from the Government of Maharashtra, followed by a BA Honours from the University of Pune. In 1980, he further honed his skills with a Post Diploma in Printmaking from the Faculty of Fine Arts at The Maharaja Sayajirao University in Baroda

Dr. Sushma Yadav

Dr. Sushma Yadav is a highly accomplished printmaker with a Ph.D. in Printmaking from the College of Arts, University of Delhi. Under the mentorship of renowned artist Anupam Sud, she has honed her skills and developed a deep understanding of printmaking techniques.

Hemavathy Guha

Hemavathy Guha received her fine art education from the College of Arts and Crafts in Chennai, India. Throughout her career, she has held 18 solo exhibitions across various parts of India and has also participated in prestigious national and international exhibitions

Kanchan Chander

Kanchan Chander is a constantly evolving artist. A recurring subject of her work is the torso - moving from the sombre and sparse look to the fun and funky one now - it's as if she has decided to just enjoy her life and her art and own it with confidence. The image of the 'body' has been a constant part of her visual lexicon. An art critic captured her perfectly - 'Driven with energy Kanchan is reclaiming the space of the creative feminine'.

Kavita Nayar

Working in this field over the past three four decades. She works in all the mediums with equal ease, be it oil or acrylic on canvas, lithography, etchings, serigraphy or pastels on paper. She is forever trying to innovate and discover herself through her works. As an artist her works evolve a sense of capturing an emotional realm of experience, individual and holistic. Her expression of art is the rendering of the intangible which permeates through the spirit of nature in which her technique is a medium to reach. The pictorial expression -'A means to an end', thus delving into the Medium of Print making as an adventurous practice that combines technique with pictorial aspects.

Rajan Shripad Fulari

Rajan Shripad Fulari is an experienced artist, curator, and art academic. He has studied Fine Arts at the Goa College of Art and completed a Masters in Printmaking from MS University, Vadodara. Fulari served as the Secretary of Lalit Kala Akademi in New Delhi and is currently an Assistant Professor at the Printmaking Studios, Faculty of Fine Arts, College of Arts and Crafts, Lucknow University.

Santosh K. Verma

Santosh K. Verma, born in Varanasi in 1956, is a talented artist known for his unique and captivating artworks. He studied at Banaras Hindu University and earned a Master of Fine Arts degree. Verma has received prestigious awards, including the National Academy Award of Lalit Kala Akademi in 2001 and the Senior Fellowship from the Ministry of Culture in 2003.

Seema Kohli

Seema Kohli is a versatile artist who explores various art forms such as visual arts, performing arts, and poetry. She has exhibited her work extensively worldwide, with over 32 solo shows and more than 300 group shows. Kohli's artistic expression revolves around themes of beauty, sensuality, and spirituality. Her work celebrates the female form and its profound connection to the creative and destructive forces of the universe. She delves into the concept of 'Hiranayagarbha' or 'The Golden Womb,' seeking to create new artistic identities by reshaping objects and engaging in a dialogue between the past and the present through decay, hybridization, and transformation.

Sushanta Guha

Sushanta Guha is a highly accomplished printmaker known for his dedication to the art form throughout his professional career. With a rare combination of formal training and practical experience, he has established himself as a prominent figure in the printmaking world.

Moti Zharotia

Moti Zharotia is renowned for his expertise in Silk Screen Printing. With a passion for art that brings him inner peace, Zharotia beautifully captures the essence of life, nature, and spirituality in his artworks. His prints showcase a stunning array of colours, employing various tools and screens to create intricate and mesmerizing designs.

V. Nagdas

V. Nagdas is known for his exceptional skills in painting and printmaking. He received his National Diploma in Painting from the College of Fine Arts in Trivandrum, Kerala, in 1982, and a Post Diploma in Graphic Arts from Viswabharati University Santiniketan in 1984.
