Featured Authors

Image Credit - Author

Chitra Banerjee Divakaruni

Chitra Banerjee Divakaruni is an esteemed Indian-American author renowned for her captivating storytelling and exploration of cultural themes. With a masterful blend of prose and poetry, she has enchanted readers worldwide.

Janice Pariat

Janice Pariat is an acclaimed author known for her diverse literary contributions. With works like "Boats on Land: A Collection of Short Stories" and "Seahorse: A Novel," she has garnered recognition and received prestigious awards, including the Young Writer Award from the Sahitya Akademi and the Crossword Book Award for Fiction.

Nilanjana S. Roy

Nilanjana S. Roy is an award-winning Indian author and journalist, known for her captivating writing style and insightful commentary. She gained critical acclaim in 2013 with her debut novel, "The Wildings," which won the Shakti Bhatt First Book Award.

Vauhini Vara

Vauhini Vara is an accomplished journalist and author known for her captivating exploration of technology, business, and culture. Her debut novel, "The Immortal King Rao", garnered critical acclaim and achieved remarkable recognition, being a Pulitzer Prize finalist, a John Leonard Prize finalist, and a notable book of the year according to NPR and The New York Times.

INTERFACES - Part 2 - LA POESIE & MUSICAL EVENING - Virtual Gallery

Interfaces - Part 2 scheduled to be held from 28-31 July will showcase exquisite prints by Olivier Deprez and Roby Comblain but will also embrace other genres such as paintings, sculpture, and mixed media with a special focus on emerging artists and women artists. It will provide an opportunity for younger artists to share the spotlight and interact with renowned artists during the exhibition.

Kerala Literature Festival - Virtual Gallery

A premier literary festival of India held annually on the beaches of Calicut. KLF is a non-aligned platform, committed to providing an open and liberal forums for debates and discussion to foster progressive discourses. KLF brings writers, thinkers and activists closer, to people of varied backgrounds and interests and empower writers, thinkers to take the liberty to exercise freedom of thought and expression.

Kolkata Literary Meet - Virtual Gallery

The Kolkata Literary Meet - or Kalam, which is their preferred daaknaam - was overwhelmingly well-received when it debuted at the Kolkata Book Fair in January 2012. Their aim is to present writers from all over the globe, writers of as many different genres as possible, alongside speakers representing India. With topics as varied as history, sport, geopolitics and cinema.
